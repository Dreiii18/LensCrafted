TIPS AND TECHNIQUES

  1. Invest in quality equipments
  2. Have the right focal point
  3. Use the rule of thirds
  4. Change your angles
  5. Pay attention to framing
  6. Use ideal lighting
  7. Use filters and settings
  8. Take lots of shots
  9. Use simple backgrounds, especially when taking portraits
  10. Take your time when editing

POST-PHOTOGRAPHY ENHANCEMENT

  1. Learn and Master your Editing Software.
  2. Adjust Exposure. Correct underexposed or overexposed areas.
  3. Adjust Contrast. Enhance the difference between the light and dark areas to make your photo more dynamic.
  4. White Balance Adjustment. Correct the color temperature to make sure whites appear truly white.
  5. Experiment with Filters and Presets
  6. Apply Sharpening for a Clearer Image, but don't overdo it to avoid unnatural looks
  7. Apply Noise Reduction Especially in Low-light Photos, but maintain a balance to avoid loss of detail.
  8. Adjust Vibrance to Enhance the Intensity of Colors Without Oversaturating.
  9. Adjust Saturation for Artistic Effects
  10. Crop your Photo to Improve the Composition and Remove Unnecessary Elements
  11. Keep it Natural. While enhancements are essential, avoid over-editing. Strive for a natural look; sometimes, less is more.
